RELIEF AT THE GRASSROOTS: DON CHRIS CUSHIONS ECONOMIC HARDSHIP WITH FREE FUEL OUTREACH

It was another moment of relief and celebration for the people of Igbo-Eze North/Udenu Federal Constituency as Engr. Chris Ugwu, fondly known as Don Chris, on 28th January, 2026, extended his ongoing humanitarian outreach to Eke Ozzi, Enugu Ezike. He distributed free fuel to motorcycle riders—both commercial (okada) operators and private owners—amid the biting economic challenges facing Nigerians.

The initiative drew a large turnout of excited beneficiaries, many of whom queued patiently to receive the much-needed support. The gesture was specifically aimed at easing the heavy burden of transportation costs, which have skyrocketed due to fuel price hikes and broader economic pressures, allowing local businesses, families, and daily commuters to breathe a little easier.

He stressed that true leadership must be felt on the ground, not just promised in speeches, and urged well-meaning individuals to always give back to society, no matter how small the act, when it touches real lives.

Reassuring the constituents, Engr. Chris Ugwu reminded everyone that the era of neglect will soon give way to responsive, people-focused representation. He added that come 2027, hope will translate into joy and tangible dividends for Igbo-Eze North/Udenu.

Beneficiaries expressed deep appreciation, offering prayers and best wishes for his aspiration. Many described him as a leader who genuinely understands the daily struggles of the grassroots and takes concrete action to alleviate them.

EDUCATION SUPPORT: DON CHRIS DISTRIBUTES OVER 3,000 EXERCISE BOOKS TO PRIMARY PUPILS ACROSS IGBO-EZE NORTH/UDENU

In line with his annual ritual of giving back to society and promoting child education, Engr. Chris Ugwu, popularly known as Don Chris, has once again empowered the future generation through his Back-to-School initiative. Over 3,000 exercise books and other essential learning materials were distributed to primary school pupils in various communities within Igbo-Eze North/Udenu Federal Constituency, Enugu State.

The program, which took place during the early year festive and resumption period, targeted indigent pupils who often face challenges accessing basic school supplies. Schools and communities saw joyful scenes as children received the books personally, with many expressing excitement about starting or continuing their academic year with the needed tools.

APC Hardship 28 January 2026

APC HARDSHIP: WHILE APC POLICIES INFLICT SEVERE ECONOMIC PAIN THROUGH SUBSIDY REMOVAL AND SOARING FUEL PRICES, DON CHRIS STEPS IN WITH REAL RELIEF FOR OUR PEOPLE

Nigeria continues to grapple with deepening economic hardship under the All Progressives Congress (APC) administration led by President Bola Tinubu. The abrupt removal of fuel subsidies in 2023, coupled with naira devaluation and persistent inflation, has driven transport costs sky-high, pushed millions into poverty, and left families struggling to afford basic mobility and essentials—even into 2026.

In Igbo-Eze North/Udenu Federal Constituency and across Enugu State, motorcycle riders (okada operators), traders, and everyday commuters bear the brunt. Fuel prices remain elevated, inflation erodes purchasing power, and many report working harder for less while food and transport expenses outpace incomes. Critics, including opposition voices like ADC leaders, argue these "vicious policies" have worsened poverty without delivering promised gains, turning what was meant as reform into prolonged suffering for the grassroots.
